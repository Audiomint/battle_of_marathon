The Persians had somewhere from 60,000 - 100,000 soldiers while the Athenians had only 9,000 - 10,000 athenians and 1,000 Plataeans. When the Athenians arrived at Marathon they blocked the two exits to get out Marathon which prevented the Persians to advance farther in. Around the same time the Athenians sent their best runner known as Pheidippides or Philippides to go to Sparta to ask for help however the Spartans were engaged in the festival of Carneia which is a sacred time of peace so they couldn't go into battle until the full moon, which meant Athens wasn't getting help for at least another 10 days. The Athenians backs were covered by many thick trees so they wouldn't get attacked from behind. The Athenians positioned themselves about 1,500 meters away from the Persians and spread out their forces so they would cover as much land in width as the Persians so they wouldn't be surrounded easily.

So the Persians were very shocked and not ready when they saw the Athenians charging at them making the first contact in the battle of Marathon.
